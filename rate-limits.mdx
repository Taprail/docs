---
title: "Rate limits"
description: "300 requests per minute per merchant by default, raisable on request."
---

Each merchant gets **300 requests per minute by default** across all `/v1/merchant/*`
endpoints combined, in both environments. The window is a fixed minute; the counter resets on
the minute boundary. If your limit has been raised (see [below](#requesting-a-higher-limit)),
that higher number applies instead.

Exceeding it returns:

```json
{
  "error": "too_many_requests",
  "message": "Rate limit exceeded (300 requests per minute). Back off and retry."
}
```

with HTTP status `429`. The number in the message reflects your current limit.

## Staying under it

300 per minute is generous for transactional traffic: that's five orders per second
sustained. Integrations that hit it are almost always polling too hard.

- **Prefer webhooks to polling.** One webhook replaces an entire polling loop.
- If you must poll, poll [Get order status](/api-reference/get-order-status) every 2 to 3
  seconds for the order you're waiting on, not every order you've ever created.
- Batch reconciliation jobs: spread them over minutes rather than firing thousands of reads
  at once, and use your own database as the primary source.

## Handling 429

Back off exponentially with jitter and retry. For order creation, keep the same
`Idempotency-Key` on the retry; see [idempotency](/idempotency).

The public checkout endpoint (`/v1/checkout/orders/:id`) is not counted against your
merchant limit; it's what the SDK polls from customers' browsers.

## Requesting a higher limit

If your traffic has genuinely outgrown 300/minute, request an increase from the dashboard
under **Developer → Rate limits**. The page shows your current ceiling and a short form:
enter the sustained requests per minute you need and a sentence on your use case. Taprail
reviews it, and once granted the new limit takes effect immediately — no key change or
redeploy.

<Note>
  You can have one open request at a time, and the page shows its status while it's under
  review. Before requesting more, confirm you're [preferring webhooks to polling](#staying-under-it)
  — that alone resolves most limits.
</Note>
