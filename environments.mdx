title: "Environments & going live"
description: "Sandbox vs live, deterministic test amounts, and the go-live checklist."

Taprail has two environments sharing one base URL — your API key decides which one you're in.

| | Sandbox (`tr_test_…`) | Live (`tr_live_…`) |
|---|---|---|
| Charges | Simulated — no money moves | Real cards, real money |
| Outcome | Deterministic, by amount (below) | The customer's real authorization |
| Payouts | Marked transferred, no actual transfer | Net settled to your payout account |
| Availability | From signup | After Taprail review |

Orders, webhooks, keys, and dashboard views are all environment-scoped: a sandbox key only
ever sees sandbox orders.

## Deterministic test amounts

In sandbox, the **last two digits of `amount_kobo`** decide the outcome, so you can exercise
every path in CI without an app in the loop:

| `amount_kobo % 100` | Outcome | `decline_reason` |
|---|---|---|
| `11` | Declined | `sandbox_declined` |
| `22` | Declined (simulated processor timeout) | `sandbox_timeout` |
| anything else | Approved | — |

```text
750000 → approved
750011 → declined (sandbox_declined)
750022 → declined (sandbox_timeout)
```

## Going live

Work through the go-live checklist on the dashboard's **Payments** page:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify your email">
    Live mode is gated on a verified address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build and test in sandbox">
    Mint a sandbox key, integrate order creation + checkout, and exercise the decline and
    expiry paths with test amounts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your webhook endpoint">
    Configure the endpoint and store both signing secrets. Confirm you verify signatures.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a payout account">
    Under **Payments → Settings**, pick your bank and verify the account — your net
    (amount minus the Taprail fee) settles there.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Taprail enables live mode">
    Once review completes, mint a `tr_live_` key and switch your server's secret.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Fees: your per-order fee is a percentage of the amount with a cap, both shown on
  **Payments → Settings**. The fee is computed when the order is created and is
  non-refundable — refunds return the full amount to the customer.
</Note>
