title: "Payouts & settlement"
description: "When you get your money, how the net is calculated, and how to reconcile."
---

When a customer pays, the full amount lands with Taprail's processor. Your **net** is the
amount minus the Taprail fee, and we transfer it to your payout account per order, usually
within a minute or two of approval.

```text
customer pays  ₦7,500.00   (amount_kobo = 750000)
Taprail fee   −₦75.00      (fee_kobo, locked at order creation)
your payout    ₦7,425.00   (one bank transfer per order)
```

## The payout lifecycle

Each order carries a `payout_state` you can read on
[Get order](/api-reference/get-order):

| `payout_state` | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `none` | The order hasn't been paid yet |
| `pending` | Paid; the transfer to your account is queued |
| `transferred` | Your net has been sent to your payout account |
| `failed` | The transfer was attempted and failed. Contact support |

A worker sweeps pending payouts about once a minute, so `pending` normally lasts seconds.

Two cases worth knowing:

- **No payout account configured.** Orders stay `pending` until you add and verify a bank
  account under **Payments → Settings**. Nothing is lost; transfers resume automatically
  once the account is in place.
- **Zero net.** If the fee consumes the full amount (tiny orders), the order is marked
  `transferred` with nothing to send.

In sandbox, payouts are marked `transferred` immediately and no money moves.

## Reconciling your bank statement

Every live payout is a separate bank transfer with a predictable reference:

```text
payout_<order_id>
```

So a statement line maps to exactly one order, and from the order you have your own
`merchant_reference`. For the charge side, `transaction_reference` on the order is the
processor's charge reference.

A simple daily reconciliation:

1. List yesterday's `approved` orders from your own database.
2. For each, confirm `payout_state` is `transferred` via the API.
3. Match `payout_<order_id>` references against your bank statement.
4. Anything `failed`, or `pending` for more than an hour, goes to a human.

## Refunds and your balance

Refunds return the **full amount** to the customer, while the fee was already taken at
charge time. The refund is recovered from your settled funds. Practically: a refunded
₦7,500 order costs you the ₦7,425 you received plus the ₦75 fee you never received.

## Fees

Your fee is a percentage with a cap, both visible under **Payments → Settings** in the
dashboard. The fee is computed once, at order creation, so the `fee_kobo` you see in the
create response is exactly what settlement uses. Changing your fee schedule later never
reprices existing orders.
