---
title: "Programs"
description: "Create, tune, pause, and budget your cashback rules."
---

A program is one cashback rule. Most partners run a single always-on program; promotions
come and go alongside it as separate programs you pause and resume.

## Creating a program

In the dashboard, go to **Programs → New program** and set four things:

1. **Name.** Customers see it with their reward ("Weekend Double Rewards"), so name it like
   a promotion, not a database row.
2. **Cashback %.** The reward rate. 2 to 5% is typical for an always-on program; promotions
   go higher for a limited budget.
3. **Cap per tap.** The most one transaction can earn. A 5% rate with a ₦500 cap means a
   ₦100,000 tap earns ₦500, not ₦5,000. Always set a cap you can live with.
4. **Budget** (optional). A total spend limit. When the program has rewarded this much in
   total, it stops matching new taps until you raise the budget or it's left as a finished
   promotion. Leave blank for unlimited.

The program starts rewarding eligible taps the moment it's created.

## How a tap earns

```text
reward = min(amount × cashback %, cap per tap)
```

A ₦8,000 tap on a 5% program with a ₦500 cap earns ₦400. A ₦15,000 tap earns ₦500, capped.
Each transaction is rewarded at most once, even if our systems see it twice.

## Editing and pausing

Everything is editable after creation: name, rate, cap, budget. Edits apply to future taps
only; rewards already earned are untouched. Pausing stops new rewards instantly and can be
reversed any time, which makes pause/resume the right tool for weekend-only or
payday-window promotions.

## Reading the numbers

- **Overview** shows the trend: cashback rewarded and rewarded taps, day by day, plus
  members and outstanding balances.
- **Rewards** is the ledger: every rewarded transaction with its program, amount, and
  reward. This is what you reconcile a budget against.

## Which transactions match

Matching is configured with you during onboarding, by your business name and merchant
category as they appear on card transactions. If a store or terminal of yours isn't
earning, or something is matching that shouldn't, contact your account manager; matching
rules aren't self-serve yet.

<Note>
  Multiple active programs: when more than one program could match a tap, the most recently
  created one wins. So launching a promotion alongside your always-on program works
  naturally: the newer promotion takes over while it's active, and pausing or exhausting it
  hands matching back to the older program.
</Note>
